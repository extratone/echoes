Event Introduction

★

To enhance Capsuleers' competitive skills and provide more opportunities for interaction and practice, EVE Echoes will host the 2024 Starbreak: Corporation League.

As this is the first competition, the official may make temporary adjustments to the schedule and rules, among other things, in response to unforeseen circumstances or force majeure factors to ensure the best experience and fairness for participating Corporations.

This League is also a trial run, marking the beginning of our efforts to make it a long-term experience. During the event, if you encounter issues or have any suggestions, please feel free to provide feedback to us, Capsuleers. Let's work together to improve the competition step-by-step.

General Description

★

1. The competition will feature 16 Corporations. Registered Corporations must have been established before October 20 and have more than 50 members.

2. In the player registration list, the top 8 teams from the latest Inscription of the Galaxy leaderboard will be prioritized, with the top 4 from each of the two leaderboards. The remaining slots will be filled by selecting highly active Corporations from the registration list and then randomly picking from them.

3. The competition adopts a single-elimination format. Teams are randomly assigned by the official draw and will compete in four stages: Round of 16, Quarterfinals, Semifinals, and the Final and Third-Place Playoff. To introduce a level of randomness, the matchups for the Quarterfinals and Semifinals matches will be re-determined randomly.

4. The competition adopts a no-loss mode, with each match being a battle between 2 Corporations. Each Corporation can send up to 30 players.

5. Each match has a fixed duration of 60 minutes. If the time limit is exceeded, the match will automatically end, and the final result will be determined based on the combat scores of both sides.

6. The schedule may be adjusted temporarily based on the final number of registered Corporations after the registration ends.

 

Competition Mode

★

After negotiating the time, both sides can initiate a Clash through the button on the event page. The Clash will enforce the Corporation's no-loss mode, with no battleship tonnage limit and 30 players per side.

1. The Clash mode requires someone with Corporation authorization to initiate and accept the match on behalf of their Corporation. Therefore, the Corporation Leaders need to negotiate and set the time at least 24 hours in advance for the Round of 16 and the Quarterfinals.

2. The Clash time must be within the dates specified by the official. The official will strictly limit the available times for each round to Saturday or Sunday. If a Clash is scheduled outside these times due to unforeseen circumstances, the official has the right to declare it invalid.

3. At the agreed time, members of both sides can enter the battlefield from Stations, Personal Citadels, and Corporation Citadels.

4. In the no-loss mode matches, players can re-enter the match multiple times after their ship is destroyed. However, be aware that as the match progresses, there will be a Cooldown Time for re-entering the battlefield, with a maximum wait time of 300 seconds. So, plan your strategy in the match accordingly.

5. If one party initiates a Clash request, but the clash has yet to take place, the inviting party may cancel the Clash and reissue the invitation.

6. Before the end of each round, the matched parties must initiate a Clash. If neither side initiates a Clash within the round, both will be considered to have forfeited and thus defeated.

7. If the invited party refuses the match or delays until the invitation expires, the inviting party will be declared the winner. If both sides successfully initiate a match but no one participates, resulting in no score changes, the inviting party will be declared the winner.

8. If both teams forfeit the previous round, resulting in an odd number of advancing teams, a bye may occur in the next round according to the random pairing rules. In this case, the team with the bye will advance directly to the next round.

9. The results and match details will be announced on the Event Page [Schedule] at the corresponding position after each round.

Supplementary Rules

★

After registration, each Corporation will elect one representative to form the "Judgment Committee." In case of disputes during the match, the remaining non-participating Corporation representatives will make decisions through voting.

 

Example:

In the match between Corporation A and Corporation B, if any disputes arise, both sides can submit evidence and recordings. The final results will be discussed and voted on by the remaining 14 representative teams and official representatives to ensure fairness. In principle, the majority vote will be the final decision.

Violation Description

Disrespecting the Referee Team

★

Players who do not respect or comply with the referee's management or decisions and do not communicate their objections to the team through proper channels.

 

Violation of Competition Ethics

★

1. Players who exhibit any insulting/inappropriate remarks/behavior during the competition period.

2. Players who make defamatory, mocking, or slanderous remarks/behaviors that damage the competition and official image during the competition.

3. Participating players who withdraw from the official matches or refuse to compete without a valid reason.

4. Any player offering bribes to any team member, management personnel, referee, or relevant personnel of another Corporation in any form.

 

Unfair Competition Behavior

★

1. Participating players who engage in any subjectively self-destructive or other negative behaviors during the competition.

2. Participating Corporations that provide false information or falsify information.

3. Exploiting game bugs: Intentionally using bugs in the game to gain an advantage. This includes all exploitation of game features that do not perform as expected and violate the design purpose of EVE Echoes.

4. Players using unauthorized programs (cheats) or unapproved programs for gaming or competition at any time or place.

5. Players who exhibit malicious behavior such as interrupting the match, obstructing the competition, or intentionally disconnecting during the match.

6. Any behavior by participating players or Corporations that manipulates the outcome or engages in collusion. This includes but is not limited to, deliberately getting eliminated, coordinating to harass other teams (malicious teaming), facilitating the advancement of a particular team (malicious teaming), and intentionally avoiding combat after a direct engagement (throwing the match).

7. Any improper operations by participating Corporations or players, both inside and outside the game, that compromise the fairness of the game are prohibited.

 

Confidentiality Obligations

★

Before the organizer officially releases competition-related information, participants and related personnel of the Corporation are not allowed to disseminate or share any competition-related rules, schedules, promotional materials, and other materials through any channels.

 

Involvement in Gambling

★

No Corporation, its members, or EVE's official personnel may directly or indirectly organize, participate in, or assist others in any betting or gambling related to the process or results of this event, nor may they directly or indirectly gain any benefits from such activities.

 

Indirect Behavior

★

No Corporation or its members shall directly or indirectly organize, induce, encourage, assist, instigate, coerce, authorize, or otherwise cause others to engage in the violations specified in these rules.

 

Penalties

★

For Corporations/players involved in violations, if the situation is severe, the results of the Corporation/player's affiliated Corporation will be canceled.

 

Right to Raise Objections

★

1. When a participating Corporation has objections to the decisions made during the competition, they can raise the objection and request a review through their management personnel to the referee team.

2. When the Corporation raises an objection, the referee team is obligated to review the objection and make a final judgment.

3. During the decision-making process, the Corporation must send a representative to speak on its behalf.

4. No objections or appeals will be accepted after the end of the match on the same day.

5. After the final judgment is announced, the Corporation may no longer raise objections or protests.

 

Competition Documents and Requirements

★

During the competition, EVE Mobile officials may request Corporations to provide documents or other reasonable items. Corporations must submit the requested documents or items on time to meet the official requirements.

 

Right to Modify Competition Rules

★

To ensure fair competition and integrity, NetEase Games reserves the right to adjust the event within the limits of the law, including but not limited to the following situations:

1. After the game client releases a new patch if the EVE Mobile official team deems it necessary to change the patch of the competition client, game settings, and items.

2. When the EVE Mobile official deems it necessary to add the latest software and features to prevent unauthorized gaming behavior.

3. When the EVE Mobile official deems it necessary to modify the game settings or rules during the online competition.
